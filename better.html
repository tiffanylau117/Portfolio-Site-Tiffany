Overview

This was a 2nd place winning project proposal from Girls in Tech-Hacking for Humanity 2022 hackathon that lasted 3 days. The design sprint was first prefaced with business problem statements and consisted of conceptualizing, brainstorming, designing, testing & then presentations. I contributed to this project as the UX/UI Designer.

About BetterMind

BetterMind is a mobile app with a personal portal for DBSA (Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance). Taking the extensive resources provided by DBSA and tailoring to create a more personal, comprehensive and accessible platform for potential users.

Problem

According to the CDC, more than 50% of Americans will be diagnosed with a mental illness or disorder at some point in their lifetime. With the rising costs of healthcare and a shortage of therapists, many people move through life untreated or even diagnosed.

It’s easy to feel helpless when the most well-known resources are out of reach. Better Mind compiles resources at no charge and offers education and options to those who don’t know where to turn. Knowledge is power and giving those suffering with poor mental health puts the power back into the hands (and minds!) of its users.

How Might We..

Motivate people to track their mental health, make changes to improve it, and find relevant resources, while being accessible?

Our Solution

  • Match users to social and volunteer events
  • Improve ease of finding relevant resources
  • Give users a space to self-reflect with a journal
  • Motivate users by setting personal goals
  • Partner with local hospitals, libraries, and shelters to distribute paper resources

    Brainstorming

    Idea vs Assumptions

    As a team, we went through 3 hours of brainstorming for the best possible, feasible product features that could serve this problem challenge: How might we remove the barrier of cost and access to care, and empower all individuals to self-manage their mental health? (DBSA)

    We also assessed DBSA's current website and their product features to get an overview of the priority of their resources. What DBSA didn't have that we could add. Also, what we could change to improve. Note: Please feel free to zoom in

    Research

    Competitor Analysis

    I conducted competitor research to understand what is currently available in the market. The strengths & weaknesses of these companies: Ginger, Moodkit, Wysa AI Chatbot, Reflectly. This is what I found.

    Qualitative Research

    We conducted user survey's to get a deeper insight on the user needs with DBSA's mental health app. These are the painpoints & opportunity that we gathered:

  • 37.5% said it was hard to find relevant mental health resources
  • 64.7% said they would be interested in staying up to date with extracurricular and volunteer events in your community
  • Personal motivation was an obstacle in improving their mental health

    • Develop

    Minimum Viable Product

    We separated the narrowed the product features down to the wants vs needs for this app. We really wanted to focus on the personal portal access so that people could self access while having all the other resources they could need in one app.

    Site Map

    I was in charge of the flow & wireframes for the onboarding process, the personal assessments and the landing homepage.

    User Flows

    Each of the flows created represents a given task/goal needed to be accomplished. For instance, event registrations, accessing resources & your mood journal.

    The main challenge with curating the flow was figuring out the navigation. We wanted to flow to be easily accessible while keeping all the features clear to the user. That is why we decide to add an early onboarding to let the users know the benefit of using this app. One thing we kept in mind was not to overwhelm the user.

    Design

    Low-Fidelity

    We split up the low-fidelity wireframes by the user flow that we created. I was in charge of the onboarding, personal assessment and user homepage. This was a challenging process because we were under time constraints.

    Visual Design

    We decide to stay true to DBSA's color scheme as well as their friendly icons. For the typography, I decided to go with a heading font that is more friendly and inviting. For the body font, I decided to go with Helvetica because it's a simple san serif & it's easy to read.

    High-Fidelity

    After getting the low-fis & the visual design down. We got a sense of how the app is mapping out, we hopped into designing the final designs.

    User Testing

    After the high-fidelity's, we created a prototype to send out to participants of the testing phase to try the app out. We found that this app is jam-packed with features and it might be quite confusing. So, we decided to add a few extra screens in the onboarding process to help them get started with the app.

    Final Designs

    Retrospective

    My thoughts and challenges I’ve faced during this project

    Thoughts

    The experience I had working with a team on a project was by far the best. I learned a lot while participating in this hackathon while not knowing what to expect because of the team dynamics and the community of Girls in Tech. I learned how to collaborate cross-functionally with people who have different perspectives and skillsets. Our team faced significant obstacles and challenges as a result of time constraints and different time zones. Because we conducted our research using the resources that DBSA already had while including new functionalities that DBSA was seeking, I thought that our group had a good proposal and solution. I've always thought that attacking the issue from its source could be the best strategy.

    Challenges

    It was challenging having such little time with each of the project deliverables. However, the most time that we took is getting the brainstorm and research down. You only get one chance to try to solve the problem statement. So, we made sure we were all on the same page. Because we were on different timezones, we divided up the tasks accordingly.

    What I learned

    I learned a significant amount when partipating in this hackathon. It taught me how to think strategically in different angles to tackle problems. What's more is that I enjoyed the collboration, learning design techniques from my teammates. I'm glad to a part of this design sprint.
